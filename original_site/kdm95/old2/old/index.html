ABOUT

I am currently working on my PhD at Cornell University with my advisor Rowena Lohman. My latest research focuses on improving methods for Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) data processing and time series analysis. InSAR can be used along with other geophysical data sets such as GPS and seismic data to understand impacts of groundwater extraction in areas like the San Joaquin valley in California. See my research page for more details about my past and current projects and my CV for more information about my professional history. Feel free to contact me with any questions or comments.
